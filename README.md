I've built The Bench — a single-file branching horror narrative from the perspective of an Oklahoma District Court judge presiding over child marriage waiver petitions.
What it includes:
 
15 unique endings based on your choices — from complicit functionary to whistleblower to resigned recusal
 
4 tracked stats: Composure, Authority, Weight (guilt), and Cases Heard
 
Atmospheric design: film grain overlay, vignette, typewriter fonts, flicker effects, and a blood-red accent palette
 
Branching structure: 3 initial choices → 9 mid-game paths → 15 distinct endings
 
Psychological horror: no jump scares or gore — the horror is in the systemic weight of judicial discretion, the silence of the statute, and the moral cost of each signature
 
Content warning at the start addressing the sensitive subject matter
The narrative arc: You begin with a single waiver petition (14-year-old, 27-year-old spouse, pregnancy cited, no counsel assigned, no statutory floor). Every choice ripples — grant it and become an efficient machine, deny it and watch the case migrate to a friendlier bench, delay it and face political pressure, or step outside the courtroom entirely to confront the architecture of the law itself.
